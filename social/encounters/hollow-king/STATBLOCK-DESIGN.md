# THE HOLLOW KING — Ashen Realm Encounter (5e Homebrew)

*He has no will left — only the echoes of everyone who ever struck him. Hit him hard enough, and he will hit you back with your own strength.*
**CR 12 · homebrew · throne-room boss · for a 4–5 person party of ~10th–12th level.**

## SIGNATURE MECHANIC — HOLLOW ECHO
The Hollow King is empty; he only reflects. **Hollow Echo (Recharge 5–6):** the King unleashes the single largest amount of damage dealt to him since his last turn, as necrotic damage, against the creature that dealt it. No attack roll — a DC 16 Constitution save halves it.
**The lesson:** don't nova. The barbarian who crits for 60 eats 60 back. Pace your damage, spread it out, and the King has nothing big to echo.

**Legendary Resistance (2/Day).** If he fails a save, he can choose to succeed instead.
**Crown of Echoes.** While crowned he is immune to psychic damage and has advantage on saves against being turned.
**The Hollow Crown (Phase 2, at 105 HP).** The crown goes dark and he rises from the throne: he gains a second attack on his Multiattack, and Hollow Echo recharges on 4–6.

### ACTIONS
**Multiattack.** Two *Ruined Greatsword* attacks (three in Phase 2).
**Ruined Greatsword.** *Melee:* +9, reach 10 ft. *Hit:* 16 (2d10 + 5) slashing plus 4 (1d8) necrotic.
**Hollow Echo (Recharge 5–6).** *See signature — reflects your biggest hit back as necrotic; DC 16 CON halves.*

### LEGENDARY ACTIONS (3)
- **Regal Step (1).** Moves up to half speed without provoking.
- **Withering Judgment (1).** One creature ≤30 ft.: DC 16 WIS save or −1d4 to its next attack roll and save.
- **Echoing Slam (2).** One *Ruined Greatsword* attack; on a hit, the target can't regain HP until the end of its next turn.

### RUNNING IT
- The throne hall is the arena (art: `art/02-throne-hall.jpg`). He begins seated — a still, dreadful tableau — and does not stir until struck or approached.
- **Emotional beat:** he never speaks and never gloats. He is a dead man reflecting the violence done to him. Players who realize he's only echoing *them* often play differently.

### READ-ALOUD
> *The king has not risen from this throne in a very long time. The crown has slipped; the eyes are only embers in an empty skull. He does not look at you. He simply waits to be struck — so that he can strike back.*
